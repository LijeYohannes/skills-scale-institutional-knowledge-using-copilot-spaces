# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Quality Assurance (QA / Testing)

### Role Summary
QA validates that deliverables meet acceptance criteria and quality standards before release.

### Responsibilities
- Create and execute test plans and test cases
- Run manual QA for complex flows and confirm acceptance criteria
- Coordinate automated test coverage and E2E checks with developers

### Typical Communication
- Report issues in the project board
- Participate in sprint planning and acceptance checks

---

## User Experience (UX) Designer

### Role Summary
Designs and validates user-facing experiences, ensuring solutions are useful, usable, and aligned to product goals.

### Responsibilities
- Produce user journeys, wireframes, prototypes, and UI specs
- Conduct usability testing and incorporate feedback
- Define accessibility and interaction requirements
- Collaborate with Product Managers to refine user problems and priorities

### Interaction Points
- Work with Product Managers to translate outcomes into tangible designs
- Collaborate with Developers on feasible implementations and acceptance
- Provide assets/specs to QA for visual and interaction checks

---

## Data Analyst

### Role Summary
Supports data-informed decision-making by defining metrics, analytics instrumentation, and analyzing outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define and track success metrics and dashboards
- Advise on event instrumentation and data collection
- Run experiments and analyze A/B test results
- Provide periodic analysis to Product Managers and stakeholders

### Interaction Points
- Partner with Product Managers to define measurable outcomes
- Coordinate with Developers on telemetry and instrumentation requirements
- Share findings in weekly syncs and iteration reviews

---

## Site Reliability Engineer (SRE)

### Role Summary
Ensures system reliability, operability, and safe deployments; owns runbooks and monitoring standards.

### Responsibilities
- Define SLOs, monitoring, and alerting requirements
- Support deployment pipelines and operational readiness checks
- Participate in incident response and post-incident reviews
- Advise on fault tolerance, scalability, and production observability

### Interaction Points
- Work with Developers on deployment and observability implementations
- Coordinate with Project Managers to include operational readiness in release checklists
- Support QA and Release owners for production verifications

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Facilitates the agile delivery process, supporting the team to execute effectively and continuously improve.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate ceremonies (standups, planning, retrospectives)
- Remove impediments and surface blockers
- Coach the team on agile practices and process improvements
- Track team health metrics and foster continuous improvement

### Interaction Points
- Coordinate with Project Managers for schedules and cross-team dependencies
- Help Product Managers and Developers align on sprint commitments
- Run retrospectives and own follow-up actions

---

## Stakeholders

### Role Summary
Individuals or groups who have interest in the project outcomes and provide inputs and approvals.

### Responsibilities
- Provide requirements, context, and approval
- Attend milestone reviews and provide feedback
- Review success metrics and outcomes post-release

---

## Interaction & Responsibility Matrix (high level)

- Product Manager ↔ UX Designer: define problem, prioritize user flows, validate designs
- Product Manager ↔ Data Analyst: set success metrics, analyze outcomes
- Project Manager ↔ SRE: schedule operational readiness, include runbooks in releases
- Project Manager ↔ Scrum Master: coordinate cadence, escalate risks and blockers
- Developers ↔ SRE: telemetry, deployment pipelines, incident response
- Developers ↔ QA: acceptance criteria and test automation coordination
- Scrum Master ↔ Team: facilitate ceremonies and continuous improvement
